\noindent Small and medium-sized distributors often manage purchasing,
warehousing, sales, invoicing, and delivery through disconnected spreadsheets,
paper documents, and manual communication. This practice leads to inaccurate
stock information, overselling, slow approval, weak traceability, and high risk
of expired goods remaining in the warehouse. This thesis proposes and implements
a web-based Smart Supply Chain Management System for distributor operations. The
system uses a layered architecture with a Spring Boot REST backend, a React
single-page frontend, and a PostgreSQL database. It integrates procure-to-stock,
order-to-cash, inventory, invoice, accounting, delivery, reporting, and
role-based administration modules in one application. The core solution maintains both
aggregate inventory and lot-level inventory with batch numbers and expiry dates.
Goods receipt creates inventory lots, sales order approval reserves available
stock to prevent overselling, and goods issue deducts stock according to the
First-Expired-First-Out principle. The system also provides a recovery mechanism
for failed delivery by restoring inventory, adjusting delivered quantity,
updating order status, and handling the related invoice state. Role-Based Access
Control is implemented for nine business roles, separating document creation,
approval, warehouse confirmation, delivery assignment, shipper execution, and
administration. The completed prototype includes 176 backend Java classes in
twelve packages, with 11,821 lines of backend source code and 10,575 lines of
frontend source code, measured with cloc excluding blank and comment lines. The main contribution of the thesis is the integration of FEFO lot
allocation, inventory reservation, failed-delivery recovery, and role-based
workflow enforcement into a focused distribution-management system.
