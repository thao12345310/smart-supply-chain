\label{appendix:usecase}
This appendix specifies additional use cases that are not central to the main
procure-to-stock and order-to-cash workflows but that complete the functional
description of the system. Tables~\ref{tab:uc-payment}, \ref{tab:uc-adjust},
and~\ref{tab:uc-explot} present the record-customer-payment, adjust-stock, and
monitor-expiring-lots use cases respectively.

\section*{Use case: Record Customer Payment}
The record-customer-payment use case registers a payment against a sales invoice
and updates the customer balance. Its specification is given in
Table~\ref{tab:uc-payment}.
\begin{table}[H]\centering
\caption{Use case specification --- Record Customer Payment}
\label{tab:uc-payment}
\begin{tabular}{|p{3cm}|p{11cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Actor} & Accountant or administrator. \\ \hline
\textbf{Pre-conditions} & A sales invoice exists; the invoice status allows payment; the payment amount is greater than zero and does not exceed the remaining amount. \\ \hline
\textbf{Main flow} & (i)~the actor opens the invoice detail; (ii)~enters the payment amount, payment method, and payment reference; (iii)~the system validates the amount; (iv)~the system updates the paid amount and the remaining amount; (v)~the invoice status becomes PARTIALLY\_PAID or PAID; (vi)~the customer balance and the sales order payment status are recalculated. \\ \hline
\textbf{Alternate flow} & The payment amount is invalid or exceeds the remaining amount: the operation is rejected and the invoice is kept unchanged. \\ \hline
\textbf{Post-conditions} & The payment is recorded; the invoice and the related sales order payment status are updated. \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\section*{Use case: Adjust Stock}
The adjust-stock use case lets warehouse staff correct an inventory record and
keeps an audit trail of the change. Its specification is given in
Table~\ref{tab:uc-adjust}.
\begin{table}[H]\centering
\caption{Use case specification --- Adjust Stock}
\label{tab:uc-adjust}
\begin{tabular}{|p{3cm}|p{11cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Actor} & Warehouse staff or administrator. \\ \hline
\textbf{Pre-conditions} & The product and warehouse exist; the actor has warehouse permission; an adjustment reason is provided. \\ \hline
\textbf{Main flow} & (i)~the actor selects an inventory record; (ii)~enters the adjustment quantity and reason; (iii)~the system validates the quantity; (iv)~the system updates the on-hand and available quantity; (v)~the system creates an inventory transaction for audit. \\ \hline
\textbf{Alternate flow} & The adjustment would make stock negative: the operation is rejected. The actor lacks permission: the request is rejected. \\ \hline
\textbf{Post-conditions} & The inventory quantity reflects the adjustment and an audit transaction is stored. \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\section*{Use case: Monitor Expiring Lots}
The monitor-expiring-lots use case gives staff visibility of stock that is close
to expiry so that they can act before loss occurs. Its specification is given in
Table~\ref{tab:uc-explot}.
\begin{table}[H]\centering
\caption{Use case specification --- Monitor Expiring Lots}
\label{tab:uc-explot}
\begin{tabular}{|p{3cm}|p{11cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Actor} & Warehouse staff, sales staff, sales manager, or administrator. \\ \hline
\textbf{Pre-conditions} & Inventory lots exist with expiry-date information. \\ \hline
\textbf{Main flow} & (i)~the actor opens the inventory lot list; (ii)~selects a product, warehouse, or expiry filter; (iii)~the system returns lots sorted by FEFO; (iv)~the actor views the lot number, remaining quantity, expiry date, days until expiry, and status. \\ \hline
\textbf{Alternate flow} & The actor opens the expiring-soon view: the system lists lots whose expiry date is within the selected threshold. The actor opens the expired view: the system lists expired lots with remaining stock. \\ \hline
\textbf{Post-conditions} & The actor obtains information for replenishment, promotion, return, or disposal decisions; no stock quantity is changed. \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}
